I was a sit-in student in Prof. Michio Kaku's class when I was 9 years old. That was the catalyst that made me fall in love with Physics and ever since then, I've been curious about the mechanics of the natural world.
I sat in Dr. Kabat's class when I was 10, and here I'm writing Maxwell's Equations in his office. You can just make out the Electric Flux symbol and Gauss' Laws. But I had no absolutely no idea what I was writing on the board back then! I was just trying to impress Dr. Kabat. And here I am, 6 years later, doing research with Dr. Kabat on none other than the relationship between Maxwell and Relativity. It took 6 years for this story to come full circle. But it would never have been possible without the endless support, encouragement, and patience of Dr. Kabat. Thank you for everything, Dr. Kabat. The research I did with Dr. Kabat became Semifinalist in the NYC Science Symposium.
